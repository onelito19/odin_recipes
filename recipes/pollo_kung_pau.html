Pollo Kung Pau

Description

Kung pao or Gong bao chicken is less known in Europe, but very popular in the United States. Adapted to the Western palate, its origin does take us to the most authentic China, in this case to the province of Sichuan, where there is a special taste for hotter and more spicy food than in other regions of the country,

Ingredients

  • Chicken breast 2
  • Soy sauce 20ml
  • Chinese vinegar or wine 20ml
  • Sherry vinager 10ml
  • Balsamic vinegar 5ml
  • Cornstarch 10g
  • pure honey 15ml
  • dried chili 6
  • Black peppercorns or Sichuan pepper 6
  • Garlic clove 3
  • fresh ginger 5g
  • Chives (green stems only) 6
  • Raw or roasted peanuts 100g
  • Extra virgin olive oil
  • Ground black pepper
  • Salt

Steps

  • 1.In a bowl, marinate the chicken with soy sauce, reice wine, and cornstarch for about 15-20 minutes
  • 2. In a separate bowl, mix together the ingredients fot the sauce until the sugar and cornstarch are dissolved/li>
  • 3. Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a wok or large skillet over high heat. Add the drid red chili peppers and stir-fry for a few seconds until they darken. Remove and set aside
  • 4. In the same wok, add the remaining 1 tablespoon of oil.Add the marinated chicken and stir-fry until it is cooked through. Remove from the wpk
  • 5. Add the peanyts to the wok and sti-fry until they start to brown slightly. then add the green onions, garlic, and ginger, and stir-fry for another minuto
  • 6. Return the chicken to wok. Give the sauce a quick re-stir and add it to the wok. Stir-fry for another couple of minutes, until the sauce has theckened and the chicken is coated
  • 7. Add the dried red chili peppers back into the wok and stir everything together
  • 8 Serve the kung pao chicken hot with steamed rice
